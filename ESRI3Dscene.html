Landslide Slope Results

Comparisons Between Public DSM and Personal Drone

The origin of the data in the top layer comes from a public agency of a city in the Puget Sound region - a Digital Surface Model created with LiDAR data. The bottom layer is elevation captured from a personal drone of the same area - ArcScene was used to do a 3D analysis to drape imagery over the elevation data calculated. Further 3D Slope Analysis was conducted to each elevation data layer, which show very different results. The symbology in each layer is identical - 4 classifications range from green to red, with red being the steepest slope. As you can see, the data provided from the drone represents more accurate slopes. In the case of identifying potential and recent landslides, the use of drones, in this case, became more valuable than the less- detailed and infrequent LiDAR data collection.
